Deftones

Deftones is an American band formed in Sacramento, California, in 1988. It is known for its unique blend of alternative metal, experimental rock, shoegaze, and atmospheric elements, creating an intense, emotional, and innovative sound.

Origin

  • The band was formed in 1988 by Chino Moreno, Stephen Carpenter, and Abe Cunningham in Sacramento. In the beginning, they played in garages and small local shows until they developed their own style, combining heaviness, melody, and atmosphere.

History

  • Deftones began gaining attention in the mid-1990s with the album "Adrenaline" (1995). After that, "Around the Fur" (1997) brought greater worldwide recognition. The major milestone of their career was "White Pony" (2000), considered one of the most important alternative metal albums.
  • Over the years, the band continued to evolve and experiment with new sounds, moving away from traditional metal patterns and creating more atmospheric and emotional music.

Trivia

  • The name “Deftones” comes from the combination of “deft” (skillful) and “tones”.
  • The band is known for mixing aggressiveness with soft melodies.
  • "White Pony" is considered one of the best albums of the 2000s.
  • Chino Moreno also participates in other musical projects such as Crosses and Team Sleep.
  • The band has strongly influenced modern metal and alternative rock.

Biography

  • Deftones is one of the most important alternative metal bands in the world. Its main members are Chino Moreno (vocals), Stephen Carpenter (guitar), Abe Cunningham (drums), and Frank Delgado (keyboards and effects).
  • Throughout their career, they have released landmark albums such as "Adrenaline", "Around the Fur", "White Pony", "Diamond Eyes", and "Ohms". Each release shows the band's evolution and its ability to reinvent its own sound.
  • Even after decades of career, the band remains active and influential, serving as a reference in modern heavy rock.
